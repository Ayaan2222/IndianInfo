Mumbai - Page 1

History: Mumbai, formerly known as Bombay, has a rich colonial past and was once an archipelago of seven islands. It became the capital of Maharashtra after the state was formed in 1960.

Overview: Known as the financial capital of India, Mumbai is home to Bollywood and boasts a vibrant cultural scene.

Places to Visit:

  • Gateway of India
  • Marine Drive
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya
  • Elephanta Caves
  • Colaba Causeway
  • Juhu Beach
  • Haji Ali Dargah

Mumbai - Page 2

History: Mumbai's transformation from a group of islands to a bustling metropolis is a testament to its strategic importance in trade and commerce.

Overview: The city never sleeps, offering a blend of modernity and tradition, from skyscrapers to historic temples.

Places to Visit:

  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya
  • Jehangir Art Gallery
  • Bandra-Worli Sea Link
  • Elephanta Caves
  • Bombay Castle

Interesting Facts:

  • Home to the world's most expensive house, Antilia.
  • Hosts the famous Ganesh Chaturthi festival.
  • Known for its diverse culinary scene, from street food to fine dining.

Delhi - Page 1

History: Delhi has served as the capital of various kingdoms and empires. It has witnessed the rise and fall of many dynasties, each leaving its mark on the city.

Overview: Delhi is a blend of ancient history and modernity, with bustling markets, historic monuments, and a vibrant culture.

Places to Visit:

  • India Gate
  • Red Fort
  • Qutub Minar
  • Humayun's Tomb
  • Lotus Temple
  • Jama Masjid
  • Chandni Chowk

Delhi - Page 2

History: Delhi was at the heart of the Indian independence movement, playing a central role in India's political and cultural evolution.

Overview: Today, Delhi serves as the capital of India and is home to both ancient sites and modern institutions.

Places to Visit:

  • Raj Ghat
  • National Museum
  • Akshardham Temple
  • Hauz Khas Village
  • Connaught Place

Interesting Facts:

  • Delhi is one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world.
  • The Delhi Metro is one of the largest and most efficient in Asia.

Kolkata - Page 1

History: Kolkata, formerly Calcutta, was the capital of British India until 1911. It is a city of cultural diversity and rich colonial history.

Overview: Known as the "City of Joy," Kolkata is famous for its intellectual and artistic culture, and it has a vibrant literary and artistic scene.

Places to Visit:

  • Victoria Memorial
  • Howrah Bridge
  • Indian Museum
  • Kalighat Temple
  • Park Street
  • Science City
  • Marble Palace

Kolkata - Page 2

History: Kolkata played a key role in India's independence movement and continues to be a hub for intellectual debates and activism.

Overview: The city blends modernity and tradition, where colonial-era architecture meets a vibrant culture of festivals, arts, and theatre.

Places to Visit:

  • St. Paul's Cathedral
  • Alambazar
  • Jorasanko Thakur Bari (Tagore House)
  • Rabindra Sarovar
  • Belur Math

Interesting Facts:

  • The famous Durga Puja festival is celebrated with grandeur in Kolkata.
  • The city is known for its traditional sweets like Rasgulla and Sandesh.
